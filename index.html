Travel nursing is a great experience for anyone that is looking for something new

It allows you to explore a new location, facility, and group of people. Contracts are on average of 13 weeks long! Besides traveling to a new location, you can look forward to gaining more work experience, the exposure to various facilities and see the unique ways they operate. Additionally, travel nurses receives a government stipend for traveling more than 50 miles outside of their permanent tax resident address which increases your earning potential!

Frequently Asked Questions

Email kle@emeraldhs.com if you've got more questions and I'll do my best to answer.

  • How many years of experience do I need to become a travel nurse?

    Typically, most facilities request 2 years of experience. Some facilities will require 3-5 years of experience depending on speciality. Because of the varying facility requirements, we advise nurses to be a bit more flexible on their first assignment.

  • What kind of experience do I need in order to start traveling?

    Acute care experience is what facilities are looking for in a travel nurse. It is very important to have hospital experience. Being a travel nurse means you will be walking into a facility with the expectation that you to be an expert on some level.

  • How long does it take to receive a California license?

    It typically takes about 2-3 months to receive a CA license as long as all the paperwork is turned in at the same time. Occasionally, I have heard cases where nurses have been lucky enough to receive their license within a month, but it is not always the case and should not be depended on. If there are any pending documents that the State needs, it most likely will prolong the license process.

  • Do you provide housing for travel nurses?

    Yes I do! I have an amazing housing coordinator that has worked for Emerald for over 10 years! There are many housing vendors that have worked with Emerald. Please keep in mind, if a nurse chooses to receive a housing package through an agency, they do not get to receive the housing stipend. I recommend that nurses find their own housing. California is a very large transit state with many professionals traveling for work — so there are many furnished housing options available.
